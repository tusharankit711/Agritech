Grams

Timing

Sowing Time

Gram is typically sown during the winter season, from October to November in India, which corresponds to the rabi season.

Temperature Requirements

Minimum Temperature

Gram requires a minimum temperature of around 10-15°C for germination.

Optimum Temperature

The ideal temperature range for gram growth is 20-25°C during the vegetative stage. Cooler temperatures during flowering (15-20°C) promote better pod setting and seed development.

Humidity Requirements

Humidity

Gram cultivation prefers moderate humidity levels. High humidity can lead to fungal diseases, so moderate humidity is ideal.

Rainfall

Rainfall Requirements

Gram is relatively drought-tolerant but requires sufficient moisture for good growth.

Rainfed Areas

Gram requires around 400-600 mm of well-distributed rainfall during the growing season.

Irrigated Areas

Proper irrigation management is crucial, especially during critical growth stages like flowering and pod formation.

Soil Requirements

Soil Type

Gram grows well in well-drained loamy soils with good organic matter content.

Fertilizers

Commonly used fertilizers for gram include nitrogenous fertilizers (urea), phosphatic fertilizers (DAP), and potassic fertilizers (MOP). Nitrogen is crucial during the early growth stages, while phosphorus promotes root development and seed formation.

Fertility

Gram requires moderate levels of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Fertilizer application should be based on soil testing and crop requirements.

Cultivation Practices

Land Preparation

Fields are plowed and harrowed to create a fine seedbed.

Sowing

Gram seeds are sown directly into the soil at the appropriate depth and spacing.

Water Management

Adequate and timely irrigation is crucial, especially during dry spells and critical growth stages.

Weed Control

Effective weed management is essential to reduce competition for nutrients and water. Herbicides are commonly used for weed control in gram fields.

Disease and Pest Management

Common pests affecting gram include pod borers, aphids, and cutworms. Diseases such as fusarium wilt and blight can also impact gram crops. Integrated pest management practices and timely application of fungicides are essential for pest and disease control.

Harvesting

Timing

Gram is harvested after about 3-4 months from sowing, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

Method

After harvesting, gram seeds are threshed and cleaned for storage or further processing.
