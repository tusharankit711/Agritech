Timing

Sowing Time

Bajra is typically sown during the summer season, around April to June in India, which coincides with the pre-monsoon and monsoon season.

Temperature Requirements

Minimum Temperature

Bajra requires a minimum temperature of around 25°C for germination and early growth.

Optimum Temperature

The ideal temperature range for bajra growth is 30-35°C during the growing season. It thrives in hot and dry climates.

Humidity Requirements

Humidity

Bajra is relatively drought-tolerant and does not require high humidity levels like rice or other crops. Moderate humidity is generally suitable.

Rainfall

Rainfall Requirements

Bajra is a drought-resistant crop and can survive with minimal rainfall.

Rainfed Areas

It can grow with as little as 400-600 mm of rainfall during the growing season.

Irrigated Areas

In irrigated areas, bajra still benefits from well-timed irrigation to ensure optimal growth and yield.

Soil Requirements

Soil Type

Bajra can grow in various soil types but prefers sandy loam to sandy soils with good drainage.

Fertility

Bajra requires moderate soil fertility. It responds well to organic matter and moderate levels of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

Fertilizers

Common fertilizers used for bajra include nitrogenous fertilizers (urea), phosphatic fertilizers (DAP), and potassic fertilizers (MOP). Fertilizer application should be based on soil testing and crop requirements.

Cultivation Practices

Land Preparation

Fields are plowed and harrowed to create a fine seedbed.

Sowing

Bajra seeds are sown directly into the soil at appropriate spacing and depth.

Water Management

While bajra is drought-resistant, timely irrigation during critical growth stages can improve yield. However, excessive moisture should be avoided, especially during flowering and grain filling stages.

Weed Control

Bajra is relatively resistant to pests and diseases compared to other crops. However, common pests include shoot fly and stem borers. Integrated pest management practices are used to manage these pests effectively.

Disease and Pest Management

Diseases such as downy mildew and rust can also affect bajra, requiring timely fungicidal treatments if necessary.

Harvesting

Timing

Bajra is harvested after about 3-4 months from sowing, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

Method

Bajra grains are harvested and dried to a safe moisture content for storage or further processing.
