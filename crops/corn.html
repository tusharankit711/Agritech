Timing

Sowing Time

Rice is typically sown in the early summer months or at the onset of the monsoon, depending on the region. In India, it's commonly sown between June and July in the kharif season (monsoon season).

Temperature Requirements

Minimum Temperature

Rice requires a minimum temperature of around 20-25°C for germination and early growth.

Optimum Temperature

Ideal temperatures for rice cultivation range between 25-35°C during the growing season. Temperatures above 35°C can adversely affect yield.

Humidity Requirements

Humidity

Rice cultivation thrives in high humidity conditions, ideally around 70-80% relative humidity during the growing period. This is crucial for pollination and grain formation.

Rainfall

Rainfall Areas

Rice is a water-intensive crop and requires substantial rainfall or irrigation.

Rainfed Areas

Typically, 1000-2500 mm of well-distributed rainfall during the growing season is ideal.

Irrigated Areas

In irrigated regions, proper water management is crucial, with periodic flooding and draining of fields.

Soil Requirements

Soil Type

Rice can grow in a variety of soils but thrives best in well-drained soils with high organic content.

pH

The ideal soil pH for rice cultivation ranges between 5.0 and 7.5.

Fertility

Rice requires good soil fertility, especially nitrogen. It's often cultivated in flooded fields where nitrogen fertilizers can be efficiently utilized.

Cultivation Practices

Land Preparation

Fields are plowed and leveled to prepare for flooding.

Sowing

Direct seeding or transplanting of young seedlings is common.

Water Management

Rice is often grown in flooded fields (paddy fields) to control weeds and pests and to ensure sufficient water availability.

Weed Control

Flooded conditions naturally suppress many weeds, but manual or chemical weed control may still be necessary.

Disease and Pest Management

Common pests include stem borers, leaf folders, and diseases like blast and bacterial leaf blight. Integrated pest management strategies are used to control these.

Harvesting

Timing

Harvesting typically occurs after 3-6 months, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

Method

2. Rice is harvested either manually or using machines like combine harvesters.

Post-Harvest

Processing

After harvesting, rice undergoes threshing, drying, and milling to produce polished rice grains.
